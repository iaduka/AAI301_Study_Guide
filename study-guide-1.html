The "Rose" Dilemma

A case study in data bias. If an AI is trained primarily on images of red roses, it learns a skewed reality, failing to represent the diversity of nature.

Hypothetical Training Data Composition

Risk: Stereotyping & Exclusion
Bias & Stereotyping

Failing to generate yellow or white roses reinforces the stereotype that "all roses are red." In human contexts, this leads to discrimination in hiring or policing.

Copyright Theft

If the AI generates a rose identical to a copyrighted painting (e.g., Van Gogh), is it creative inspiration or data theft?

Hallucination

The AI might combine features to create a "Sunflower-Rose" hybrid and present it as real, spreading misinformation about biology.

Mitigation

Requires diverse datasets (including all rose colors) and RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) to correct skew.
